About

I am a first-year doctoral researcher at the Technical University of Munich, working on time series analysis, spatio-temporal data mining, and Urban AI. I am supervised by Prof. Dr. Ziyue Li at TUM and Dr. Chenxi Liu at the Chinese Academy of Sciences.

My current research centres on the Urban Heat Island Foundation Model (UHIFM), through which I am developing a global, 16-city, hourly pretraining dataset and benchmark for UHI modelling. More broadly, I aim to build generalizable urban climate foundation models that connect environmental, socio-economic, and health-related data with climate simulation and real-time urban decision support.

Research

Three Threads

01 ·

Spatio-Temporal Foundation Models

Foundation-model-based methods for hourly urban climate forecasting, attribution, and cross-city transfer — centred on the Urban Heat Island Foundation Model (UHIFM).

02 ·

Interpretable Urban Climate AI

Interpretable ML for urban heat exposure, climate equity, and adaptive mitigation strategies — linking SHAP attribution, scenario simulation, and Bayesian optimisation.

03 ·

Multimodal Urban Understanding

Semantic urban mapping, POI retrieval, and vibe-aware place discovery from spatial, textual, and visual signals — beyond category-based urban classification.
